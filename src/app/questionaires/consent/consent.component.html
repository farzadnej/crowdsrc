Study Name: Crowd Sourcing - Modeling Quality of Experience in Online Video Playback

Researchers:
Dr. M. Chignell, Mechanical and Industrial Engineering, University of Toronto
Mr. James Bond, Mechanical and Industrial Engineering, University of Toronto

Research Rationale: Contribute to the advancement of the understanding of Quality of Experience (QoE), a measure of a customer's experiences with a service, through its design and management within real networks by developing models to better assess and predict user evaluations of the QoE during video services.

Research Procedure:
You are invited to voluntarily take part in this study and will be rewarded $20 for your time. You are free to refuse to participate or withdraw from the study at any time without penalty.

This experiment will take approximately one hour and half and there is no foreseeable risk or harm to you. You will be asked to experience three sessions of video watching and fill out questionnaires. You will receive an optional five-minute break after session 2.

The study is industry-sponsored; the industry sponsor will not have access to the data collected. All data collected will be coded to protect identity and kept in a secure office. No personal or identifying information will be included in written reports or presentations and all data will be kept in a locked office and on a password encrypted computer, accessible by only the investigators. Furthermore, the data will be securely stored until September 1st, 2021, after which it will be destroyed.
